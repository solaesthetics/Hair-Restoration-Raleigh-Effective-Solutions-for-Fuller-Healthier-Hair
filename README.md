## Why Hair Loss Happens

Hair loss develops for many reasons, and the right solution depends on identifying the underlying cause. Some of the most common factors include:

* Genetic predisposition (androgenetic alopecia)
* Hormonal fluctuations
* Aging
* Nutritional deficiencies
* Stress-related shedding
* Medical conditions
* Certain medications
* Poor scalp health

According to the American Academy of Dermatology, it is normal to lose between **50 and 100 hairs per day** as part of the natural hair growth cycle. However, excessive shedding or noticeable thinning may indicate a condition that requires professional evaluation.

## Understanding the Hair Growth Cycle

Healthy hair follows a continuous cycle consisting of three primary phases:

### Anagen (Growth Phase)

This active growth stage lasts approximately **2 to 7 years**. Around **85–90% of scalp hairs** remain in this phase at any given time.

### Catagen (Transition Phase)

A brief stage lasting two to three weeks where the hair follicle gradually shrinks.

### Telogen (Resting Phase)

The follicle rests before the hair naturally sheds and a new strand begins growing.

When this cycle becomes disrupted, thinning hair and reduced density become more noticeable over time.

## Why Early Treatment Matters

One of the biggest misconceptions about hair loss is waiting until significant thinning has already occurred.

Hair follicles gradually become weaker over time. Addressing concerns during the early stages can often help preserve existing hair while encouraging healthier growth patterns.

Benefits of early intervention include:

* Better preservation of existing follicles
* Improved hair density
* More natural-looking results
* Reduced progression of thinning
* Greater long-term treatment success

A professional assessment allows specialists to determine which follicles remain active and how they can best be supported.
